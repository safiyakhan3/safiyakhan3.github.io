Why This Project Exists ✈️🍔🌄

Hi! I’m Safiya, a college student who learned first-hand that exploring the U.S. isn’t just for people with unlimited money or time. This site is my proof that adventure can be affordable—and that planning smarter (not pricier) gets you further.

I built this blog to practice semantic HTML, clean CSS, and accessibility—and to share real, budget-friendly ways students can see more of the world without burning out their wallets. If one person takes a trip because of something here, that’s a win. 🙌

Why Travel Matters for Students 🧭

  • It builds confidence and independence—navigating new cities = life skills.
  • It grows perspective—you meet new people, cultures, and ideas, not just landmarks.
  • It teaches resourcefulness—budgeting, planning, adapting, problem-solving.
  • It sparks curiosity & creativity—you return with new stories and better questions.

Travel shouldn’t be a luxury item. It’s an education. And students deserve access to it. Full stop.

What We Need: Better Tools for Affordable Travel 💡

Most travel sites are optimized for people with high budgets or tons of time. Students need tools that are honest about trade-offs and optimized for value. Imagine platforms that:

  • Show bus/train vs. car vs. budget airline comparisons in one view 🚍🚆✈️
  • Prioritize student discounts, shoulder-season dates, and hidden-gem neighborhoods 🎟️📅🗺️
  • Bundle cheap stays (hostels, Airbnbs, university housing), grocery + food hacks, and free activities 🛏️🥪🌳
  • Surface crowd-vetted itineraries from young travelers, not just influencers 👥

This project is a small step in that direction: clear information, transparent trade-offs, and routes anyone can copy.

How to Use This Site 🧰

  1. Home: my origin story + what you’ll find.
  2. Destinations: quick highlights + a visual grid to spark ideas.
  3. Themes: deep-dives (like affordable food across California). 🍜🔥
  4. Tips: student-friendly planning tricks, sites, and budget tools.
  5. Contact: say hi, trade recs, or share your own routes.

Key Terms 🧩

HTML
Gives structure and meaning to web content through semantic elements.
CSS
Controls presentation—fonts, colors, layout, spacing—separate from content.
Alt Text
Short, descriptive text that makes images accessible to screen-reader users.

Accessibility is part of good design—not an afterthought. ♿️

Goal: help more students travel farther for less—then share what works so others can follow. 🔄
